\begin{abstract}
We establish the Spacetime Penrose Inequality under explicitly delineated conditions, clearly distinguishing between different levels of assumptions.

\textbf{Main Result (Theorem~A---Conditional Penrose Inequality):} For asymptotically flat initial data $(M^3,g,k)$ satisfying the dominant energy condition, let $\Sigma$ be a closed trapped surface. The inequality
\[M_{\mathrm{ADM}}(g) \ge \sqrt{A(\Sigma)/(16\pi)}\]
holds if we assume the \textbf{Favorable Jump Condition}: $\Sigma$ is a MOTS and $\tr_\Sigma k \ge 0$.
Equality holds if and only if the data embed isometrically into the Schwarzschild spacetime. The proof utilizes a \textbf{Jang Reduction for MOTS} that solves the generalized Jang equation with blow-up forced at the MOTS, combined with the $p$-harmonic level set method.

\textbf{Alternative Assumptions (Theorem~B):} If the favorable jump condition is not assumed directly, the inequality holds under either:
\begin{itemize}
    \item[(i)] \textbf{Compactness:} The outermost MOTS $\Sigma^*$ satisfies the favorable jump condition (which is plausible but not guaranteed by stability alone) and encloses $\Sigma$ with $A(\Sigma^*) \ge A(\Sigma)$; OR
    \item[(ii)] \textbf{Cosmic Censorship:} The data embed in a spacetime satisfying weak cosmic censorship, ensuring the existence of an event horizon with area at least $A(\Sigma)$.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Clarification on Stability:} While the outermost MOTS is stable ($\lambda_1 \ge 0$), we clarify that stability alone is \emph{insufficient} to guarantee the favorable jump condition $\tr_\Sigma k \ge 0$ without additional gauge assumptions. \textbf{Warning:} The condition $\tr_\Sigma k \ge 0$ is NOT automatic even for stable MOTS. Thus, the result is presented as conditional.

\textbf{Open Problem:} For general trapped surfaces with $\tr_\Sigma k < 0$, the inequality remains open without cosmic censorship. We discuss the obstructions to extending the Jang reduction to this case.
\end{abstract}
