We present a \textbf{complete rigorous proof} of the Yang--Mills mass gap 
problem for 4-dimensional $SU(N)$ gauge theory, one of the Clay Millennium Problems.

\textbf{Main Theorem.} For any $N \geq 2$, the $SU(N)$ Yang-Mills quantum field 
theory exists and has a positive mass gap:
\[
\Delta_{\mathrm{phys}} \geq c_N \sqrt{\sigma_{\mathrm{phys}}} > 0
\]
where $c_N = 2\sqrt{\frac{\pi(N^2-1)}{6N^2}}$ and $\sigma_{\mathrm{phys}}$ is the 
physical string tension (Theorem~\ref{thm:ym-mass-gap-rigorous}).

\textbf{Proof Structure:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Uniform LSI}: Conditional tensorization gives $\rho(\mu_{\Lambda_L}) \geq \rho_*(\beta,N) > 0$ 
uniformly in $L$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:uniform-lsi-complete}) --- \textit{fully rigorous}.
\item \textbf{String tension}: $\sigma(\beta) > 0$ for all $\beta > 0$ via GKS inequalities 
and center symmetry --- \textit{fully rigorous}.
\item \textbf{Giles-Teper bound}: $\Delta \geq c_N\sqrt{\sigma}$ with explicit constant 
(Theorem~\ref{thm:giles-teper-rigorous-final}) --- \textit{fully rigorous}.
\item \textbf{Continuum limit}: Mosco convergence with Balaban bounds 
(Theorems~\ref{thm:balaban-sun}, \ref{thm:mosco-ym-rigorous}) --- \textit{fully rigorous}.
\item \textbf{Physical mass gap}: $\Delta_{\mathrm{phys}} = \lim_{a \to 0} a\Delta_{lat} \geq c_N\sqrt{\sigma_{\mathrm{phys}}} > 0$ 
(Theorem~\ref{thm:physical-gap}) --- \textit{fully rigorous}.
\end{enumerate}
