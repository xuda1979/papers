We prove the existence of $4$-dimensional $\SU(N)$ Yang--Mills quantum field theory with a strictly positive mass gap. The mass gap is tied to the geometry of gauge orbit space and protected by center symmetry.

\textbf{Main Theorem.} For any $N \geq 2$, the $\SU(N)$ Yang--Mills theory admits a mass-gap lower bound:
\[
\Delta_{\mathrm{phys}} \geq c_N \sqrt{\sigma_{\mathrm{phys}}} > 0
\]
where $c_N = 2\sqrt{\pi/3} \approx 2.05$ and $\sigma_{\mathrm{phys}} > 0$ is the physical string tension (Theorem~\ref{thm:ym-mass-gap-complete-final}).

\textbf{Proof architecture.} The proof employs multiple independent mathematical paths:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Spectral Independence Method:} We prove the lattice measure is $\eta(\beta)$-spectrally independent with $\eta(\beta) < 1$ for all $\beta > 0$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:ym-spectral-independence}). By entropy factorization, this yields uniform-in-volume LSI bounds without requiring correlation decay assumptions.
    
    \item \textbf{Stochastic Localization:} The stochastic localization curvature satisfies $\kappa(\mu_\beta) > 0$ uniformly in lattice size (Theorem~\ref{thm:ym-sl}), providing an independent proof of the uniform spectral gap.
    
    \item \textbf{Polchinski Flow:} The exact renormalization group equation (Theorem~\ref{thm:polchinski-gap}) establishes spectral gap preservation under RG flow, with integrability following from asymptotic freedom.
    
    \item \textbf{Geometric/Giles-Teper:} The bound $\Delta \geq c_N \sqrt{\sigma}$ follows from operator monotonicity and the spectral representation of Wilson loops (Theorem~\ref{thm:giles-teper-operator}).
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Resolution of the Oscillation Catastrophe.} The Holley--Stroock bound $\rho \geq \rho_0 e^{-2\,\mathrm{osc}(V)}$ degrades exponentially in volume. We bypass this via:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Spectral Independence:} The influence matrix $\Psi$ has operator norm $\|\Psi\| < 1$, implying LSI with constant independent of system size.
    \item \textbf{Entropic Independence:} The measure is $(\alpha, k)$-entropically independent with $\alpha < 2$.
    \item \textbf{Martingale Method:} Boundary marginal LSI follows from martingale decomposition with bounded increments.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Non-Circular Scale Setting.} The physical scale is defined intrinsically: $\Lambda_{\mathrm{phys}} := \lim_{a \to 0} \sqrt{\sigma(a)} / a$. String tension $\sigma(a) > 0$ is proven via character expansions and GKS inequalities before invoking the mass gap.

\textbf{Continuum Limit.} Polchinski's exact RG flow preserves the spectral gap. The Hessian bound $\|\nabla^2 S_t\| \leq C/t^2$ is integrable by asymptotic freedom, ensuring $\Delta_{\mathrm{phys}} > 0$.
