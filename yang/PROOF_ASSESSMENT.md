# Rigorous Assessment of QCD Mass Gap Proof

## What We Have Proven

### QCD_RIGOROUS_PROOF.tex - The Argument

**Theorem**: SU(3) QCD with $N_f = 2$ and $m_q > 0$ has a mass gap.

**Proof Strategy**:
1. Vafa-Witten: Only χSB is allowed (rigorous)
2. 't Hooft anomaly matching: UV anomalies must be matched in IR
3. No-go theorem: No massless fermions can match anomalies
4. Conclusion: χSB must occur
5. GMOR: $m_\pi > 0$ for $m_q > 0$

## CRITICAL ASSESSMENT: Is This Truly Rigorous?

### Rigorous Components ✓

1. **Vafa-Witten theorem** - Published 1984, peer-reviewed, accepted by community
   - Uses reflection positivity
   - Constrains which symmetries CAN break

2. **'t Hooft anomaly matching** - Published 1980, universally accepted
   - Anomalies are 1-loop exact
   - UV and IR must match

3. **GMOR relation** - Ward identity consequence, rigorous
   - $m_\pi^2 f_\pi^2 = (m_u + m_d)|\langle\bar{q}q\rangle| + O(m_q^2)$

4. **Chiral Ward identities** - Rigorous mathematical framework

### Potential Gap ⚠️

**The No-Go Theorem (Section 5)**: 

The argument that "massless composite fermions cannot match anomalies" is:
- **Physically very compelling** - widely believed by experts
- **Used in literature** - 't Hooft's original paper used similar reasoning
- **BUT**: Not proven with mathematical rigor comparable to Vafa-Witten

**The issue**: We enumerate baryon representations and show they can't match. But:
- Have we covered ALL possible composites?
- What about exotic states?
- Is there a mathematical proof that the enumeration is complete?

### 't Hooft's Original Argument

't Hooft (1980) made essentially this argument:
- If chiral symmetry is unbroken, need massless fermions
- Baryons are the only candidates
- They can't match $\to$ χSB must occur

This is accepted physics wisdom, but not a mathematical theorem.
